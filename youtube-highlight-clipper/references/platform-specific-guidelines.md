# Platform-Specific Guidelines for Short-Form Content

## TikTok Algorithm Preferences (2024)

### First Second Criticality
- 0-0.5 seconds: Must contain visual hook OR audio hook
- 0.5-1.5 seconds: Establish value proposition or curiosity gap
- 1.5-3.0 seconds: Deliver on promise or deepen intrigue

### Content Characteristics That Perform Well
- Vertical format (9:16 aspect ratio)
- Text overlay on key phrases (especially in first 3 seconds)
- Trending sounds or original audio with clear rhythm
- Jump cuts every 2-3 seconds to maintain pace
- Captions burned into video (85% watch without sound)

### Optimal Length by Content Type
- Entertainment/Dance: 7-15 seconds
- Educational/Tips: 15-30 seconds  
- Story/Narrative: 21-35 seconds
- Product Demo: 9-18 seconds

## Instagram Reels Algorithm Preferences

### First 3 Seconds Weighting
- Heavily favors content that stops scrollers
- Aesthetic quality matters more than TikTok
- Faces in first frame get 23% more initial engagement

### Content Characteristics That Perform Well
- Slightly polished production quality acceptable
- Lifestyle, beauty, fitness niches strong
- Educational content performs better with 15-30s range
- Strong call-to-action in final 2 seconds (follow, visit link)

### Optimal Length by Content Type
- Lifestyle/Fashion: 9-15 seconds
- Fitness/Dance: 15-25 seconds
- Educational/Cooking: 20-40 seconds
- Behind-the-Scenes: 13-20 seconds

## YouTube Shorts Algorithm Preferences

### First 2 Seconds Strategy
- Can leverage channel branding and trust
- Educational hooks with authority perform well
- "You won't believe..." style openings effective

### Content Characteristics That Perform Well
- Can be slightly longer (up to 60 seconds still considered Short)
- Tutorial/how-to content exceptionally strong
- Leverages YouTube search discovery
- Comments and engagement weight heavily in distribution

### Optimal Length by Content Type
- Tutorials/Education: 30-50 seconds
- Commentary/Reaction: 15-30 seconds
- Clips/Highlights: 10-25 seconds (matches our skill constraints)
- Vlogs/Daily Life: 15-30 seconds

## Cross-Platform Optimization Principles

### Universal Hook Elements
1. **Specificity**: Numbers, names, concrete details outperform vague statements
2. **Emotion**: Strong feelings (surprise, anger, joy, fear) drive shares
3. **Relevance**: Ties to current events, trends, or universal experiences
4. **Utility**: Clear benefit or skill gained from watching

### Technical Specifications for All Platforms
- Resolution: 1080x1920 (minimum 720x1280)
- Format: MP4 with H.264 codec
- Frame rate: 24-30fps (consistent throughout)
- Audio: AAC, 128kbps minimum, stereo preferred
- File size: Under 100MB for reliable upload

### Caption Best Practices
- Burn captions directly into video (not just platform captions)
- Use large, readable fonts (minimum 4% of video height)
- High contrast text (white with black outline or vice versa)
- Sync captions precisely with speech timing
- Include sound effect descriptions when relevant ([LAUGH], [MUSIC], etc.)

## Retention Curve Optimization

### The Critical Drop-off Points
- 0-3 seconds: Hook effectiveness (aim for <20% drop-off)
- 3-8 seconds: Value delivery (aim for <35% cumulative drop-off)  
- 8-15 seconds: Main content (aim for <50% cumulative drop-off)
- 15+ seconds: Conclusion/CTA (aim for <65% cumulative drop-off for good performance)

### Engagement Boosters by Timestamp
- 0-2 seconds: Visual surprise or text pattern interrupt
- 4-6 seconds: First emotional peak or revelation
- 9-12 seconds: Secondary insight or example
- 14-16 seconds: Call-to-action or cliffhanger (for series)

## Testing and Iteration Framework

### Quick Validation Methods
- Show hook to 5 people: Do they want to see more?
- Audio-off test: Is visual hook still compelling?
- Speed test: Does it work at 2x playback speed?
- Crowding test: Does it stand out in a busy feed?

### Iteration Priority Order
1. Hook strength (non-negotiable)
2. Context completeness (can standalone)
3. Emotional resonance 
4. Information density
5. Perfect timing (secondary to quality)

Remember: A slightly longer clip with an amazing hook beats a perfectly timed clip with a weak hook.
